# Privacy Basics

Bitcoin is public by default. Identities are not directly stored, but transaction graphs are visible to everyone.

## Core Idea

Privacy in Bitcoin means reducing how easily someone can link addresses, transactions, and behavior back to you.

## Simple Analogy

Imagine wearing different outfits every day (new addresses), but always taking the same route and paying exact same amount. People can still recognize you from patterns.

On-chain privacy works the same way.

## Common Privacy Leaks

- Reusing addresses.
- Merging many old UTXOs in one spend.
- Predictable change outputs.
- Unique payment amounts and timing patterns.

## Better Habits

- Use fresh addresses.
- Avoid unnecessary UTXO merges.
- Prefer wallets with good privacy defaults.
- Understand what your wallet reveals when building transactions.
